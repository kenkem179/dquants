# UTC-21 thin-window blocking study — KK-MasterVP XAU M5 (2026-06-24)

## Motivation
After the pure-UTC session migration, the Asia session starts at UTC 21:00 (JST 06:00). User noted
Exness XAU is "basically closed" at JST 06:00. Bar data confirms a daily break:

| UTC hour | JST | M5 bars (normal ≈ 4,356) |
|---|---|---|
| 20 | 05:00 | 4,212 |
| **21** | **06:00** | **1,536 (~35%)** ← Exness XAU daily break |
| 22 | 07:00 | 2,692 (~62%) |
| 23 | 08:00 | 4,316 |

The few entries at UTC 21 (8 over 4 months) are all at 21:55 (thin-liquidity). The validated lock
already traded this window, so it is not a regression — but it is a low-quality window.

## Method
6-fold walk-forward (`research/mastervp_parity/wf_mastervp.py`, `--set-all`, folds F1..F6 over the
continuous XAU tick file), comparing `InpBlockedHoursStr` candidates. `InpBlockedHoursStr` is a real
EA input → any winner ships via `.set`, no recompile.

## Results

| InpBlockedHoursStr | n | win% | PF | net | maxDD | folds+ | worstPF |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **4,16,17 (lock)** | 1352 | 59.1 | **1.344** | **23,098** | **7.8%** | 6/6 | **1.223** |
| 4,16,17,21 | 1342 | 58.9 | 1.303 | 19,784 | 8.7% | 6/6 | 1.223 |
| 4,16,17,22 | 1329 | 59.4 | 1.340 | 22,276 | 7.6% | 6/6 | 1.166 |
| 4,16,17,21,22 | 1318 | 59.2 | 1.304 | 19,363 | 8.6% | 6/6 | 1.166 |

## Verdict — REJECT (keep `4,16,17`)
- Blocking **21** HURTS: PF 1.344→1.303, net −14%, maxDD worse (7.8→8.7%). The thin-window trades it
  removes are sparse but net-positive in backtest.
- Blocking **22** is ≈ net-neutral (slightly lower net, marginally lower pooled dd) but **degrades the
  worst fold** (1.223→1.166) → fails the T1 "don't degrade worst fold" rule.
- Baseline `4,16,17` ranks #1 on robustness (folds+ 6/6, pooled PF, worst-fold PF). No new lock → no
  overfitting-gate run needed (candidate fails the engine WF outright).

## Caveat
The engine fill model may be over-optimistic on the thin UTC-21 break bars (wider real spreads / slippage /
gaps). "Don't block" is the *backtest* verdict; execution quality at the daily break is a live-safety
consideration, not a backtest-supported edge. If live fills at 21:55 look poor on the demo forward-test,
revisit as a live-safety veto (not a profitability lever).
