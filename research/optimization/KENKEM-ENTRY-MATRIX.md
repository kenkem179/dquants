# KenKem entry-matrix results — all individual entries & combinations

Full performance matrix for every individually-toggleable KenKem entry and every tuned combination,
on the distilled `kk::kenkem` C++ engine. Per-entry knobs tuned in isolation (phaseA), shared gates
re-tuned per combo (phaseB), all ranked on **2026 true out-of-sample**. Generated by
`research/optimization/report_kenkem.py` replaying each saved `.set` (native trail+partial ON, common
ProfitManager OFF). Costs modelled (BTC spread $2.0, XAU spread $0.05).

**Sort order:** net profit ↓, then max-DD% ↑, then profit factor ↓.

**MaxDD%** = engine dollar drawdown ÷ $10,000 starting balance (fixed-fraction sizing on $10k). It is a
proxy — because the balance compounds, dollar DD against the *starting* balance slightly overstates the
true late-period %. Recovery factor = net ÷ maxDD (dollars). Sharpe = annualized daily-PnL Sharpe
(365 for BTC, 252 for XAU). Trades/day over the test span.

> **Timeframe scope:** two timeframes below, each **independently tuned**. **M1** = engine runs entries
> on the M1 bar with M3/M5/M15 as higher-TF confirmation. **M3** = a dedicated re-tune with the M3 bar as
> the engine base (so the fixed ×3/×5/×15 aggregation shifts confirmation to M9/M15/M45); all per-entry
> knobs + shared gates were re-optimised on M3 2025 and validated on M3 2026 OOS — NOT M1 params replayed.
> The EMA/ADX *shape* periods are held at production values on both TFs. M3 trades far less (≈⅓ the bars),
> so M3 nets are structurally lower than M1 — compare TFs on PF / MaxDD% / Sharpe, not raw net.

## Reading the matrix

- **Net-profit sort favors the widest combos** (E1+E2+E5 tops every window) — they simply trade more.
  That is also where MaxDD% climbs and PF/Sharpe dilute.
- **By quality keys, E5-only wins outright** on both symbols, both windows: lowest MaxDD% (11–25%),
  highest PF (1.62–1.79), best Sharpe — it just trades least, hence lowest net. The indispensable core.
- **E2+E5 is the best net+quality blend**: ~1.8× E5's net while holding PF ≈1.35 and the best recovery
  factor among multi-entry combos (BTC OOS 40.8, XAU OOS 26.8).
- **E4 and E1 are the weak legs**: bottom on MaxDD% (E4 OOS 74–77%, E1 OOS 80%) and PF. E1 OOS PF 1.071
  (BTC) is barely above breakeven; E4 degrades OOS on XAU.

## Scope / provenance

- 9 configs: 4 singles (E1, E2, E4, E5) + 5 combos (E1+E2, E1+E5, E2+E5, E1+E2+E5, E4+E5). E3 is absent
  in the distilled engine. Combos without E5 beyond E1+E2, and E1/E2+E4 blends, were not part of this
  per-entry-tuned study (see `KENKEM-RESULTS.md` entry-set comparison for PF/net-only coverage of more
  combos).
- Per-entry frozen knobs: `best_tuned_<e>_{btc,xau}.json` / `.set`. Combo configs:
  `best_kenkem_<combo>_{btc,xau}.set`. Report tool: `report_kenkem.py` + `report_metrics.py`.
- Engine: `cpp_core/include/kk/kenkem/*.hpp`. Bars: `cpp_core/tools/bars_{btcusd,xauusd}_{2025,2026}_m1.csv`.

## Cross-timeframe reading (M1 vs M3)

- **E5-only is the quality king on BOTH timeframes** — lowest MaxDD% (M3 OOS: 23% BTC / 8% XAU) and
  highest PF (M3 OOS 1.44 BTC, 1.67 XAU; M3 IS hits 1.93 on XAU), at the cost of the fewest trades and
  lowest net. The indispensable core regardless of TF.
- **E4 (Ichimoku) prefers M3.** On M1 it was a drag (XAU OOS PF 1.168, worst MaxDD%); on M3 it is a
  *leading* leg — **E4+E5 is the single best combo on both symbols' M3 OOS** (BTC PF 1.370, XAU PF
  1.320). The Tenkan/Kijun cross is cleaner on the slower bar.
- **E2 (pullback) prefers M1.** Robust on M1 XAU (OOS PF 1.33), but on M3 XAU it collapses to breakeven
  (OOS PF 1.008 solo, and E2+E5 is the *worst* XAU-M3 combo at 1.097 / 79% MaxDD). E2's touch logic needs
  the M1 granularity.
- **Net is structurally lower on M3** (≈⅓ the bars → fewer entries). Do not compare M1 vs M3 on raw net;
  the PF/Sharpe/MaxDD% columns are the fair cross-TF comparison.
- **Production implication:** if running M3, prefer **E4+E5** (or E5-only for max quality); if running M1,
  prefer **E2+E5** (or E5-only). The best second leg flips with timeframe — E4 on M3, E2 on M1.
